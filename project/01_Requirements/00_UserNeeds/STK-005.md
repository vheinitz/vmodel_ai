# Easy Cleaning and Cross-Contamination Prevention

## Need

The device shall be easy to clean between different samples to prevent cross-contamination. Surfaces that may contact liquids or aerosols during operation must be accessible, smooth, and resistant to standard laboratory disinfectants. The cleaning procedure must be quick and simple enough to be performed between every sample without disrupting lab workflow.

## Context

In a medical environment, cross-contamination is a critical failure mode. If residue from one patient sample contaminates the next, the resulting diagnostic error could lead to patient harm. Cleaning must be practical for high-throughput daily use — a procedure that takes too long or requires disassembly will be skipped by busy staff.

## Acceptance

Laboratory staff will consider this need met when:
- All surfaces potentially exposed to sample material are reachable without tools
- Standard lab disinfectants (e.g., 70% ethanol, sodium hypochlorite solution) can be used without damaging the device
- The cleaning procedure takes no more than 2 minutes between samples
- No residual biological material is detectable after following the cleaning procedure
