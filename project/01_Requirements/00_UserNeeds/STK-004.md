# Operator Safety — Spill, Splash, and Injury Prevention

## Need

The device shall prevent spills, splashes, and mechanical injury during normal operation. Laboratory staff must be able to operate the mixer without risk of exposure to potentially infectious sample material. Containers must be enclosed or shielded during mixing so that any accidental container failure does not expose the operator.

## Context

Medical laboratory samples may contain bloodborne pathogens (HIV, HBV, HCV) or other infectious agents. Even minor splashes pose a serious occupational health risk. Safety must be designed in — relying solely on operator training or written warnings is not acceptable for a device handling biohazardous material.

## Acceptance

Laboratory staff will consider this need met when:
- No liquid escapes the mixing area during normal operation
- Containers are shielded or enclosed during the mixing cycle
- Operators cannot access moving parts during operation
- No sharp edges or pinch points are present on accessible surfaces
