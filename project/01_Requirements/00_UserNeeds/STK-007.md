# Operational Status Indication

## Need

The device shall provide a clear signal — visual or audible — indicating the current operational state: mixing in progress, mixing complete (done), or an error/fault condition. The signal must be perceivable from a distance without requiring the operator to be standing directly in front of the device.

## Context

Lab staff typically manage multiple instruments simultaneously. An unambiguous status signal lets them start a mixing cycle, attend to another task, and return when the cycle is complete — or respond immediately if an error occurs. A device with no status indication would require constant visual monitoring, wasting staff time.

## Acceptance

Laboratory staff will consider this need met when:
- Mixing-in-progress, completed, and error states are each indicated by a distinct, easily distinguishable signal
- The signal is visible or audible from at least 3 meters away in a typical lab environment
- The "done" signal is clearly different from the "in-progress" signal (no ambiguity)
- Error indication draws immediate attention (e.g., flashing or audible alarm)
