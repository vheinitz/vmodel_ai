# Rule: Risk Management Document Structure (ISO 14971 / Johner Institut)

## Purpose
This rule defines how risk management artifacts must be structured, what they must contain, and how they trace to requirements — per ISO 14971:2019 methodologies as taught by the Johner Institut and FDA CDRH guidance.

## 2. Risk Management Plan (RMP) — Required Content

Per ISO 14971:2019 §4.4, the RMP must contain:

- [ ] Scope of risk management activities (which product, lifecycle phases)
- [ ] Assignment of responsibilities and authorities
- [ ] Requirements for review of risk management activities
- [ ] Criteria for risk acceptability (the acceptance matrix)
- [ ] Method for evaluation of overall residual risk
- [ ] Verification activities for risk control measures
- [ ] Activities for collection and review of production and post-production information

### RMP Template Structure
```markdown
# Risk Management Plan
## 1. Scope
## 2. Responsibilities
## 3. Risk Acceptability Criteria (include acceptance matrix)
## 4. Risk Analysis Methodology (FMEA, FTA, HAZOP — which for what)
## 5. Risk Evaluation Method
## 6. Risk Control Methodology (hierarchy)
## 7. Verification of Risk Controls
## 8. Overall Residual Risk Evaluation
## 9. Post-Market Activities
## 10. Review Schedule
```

## 3. FMEA Entry Structure

Every FMEA entry MUST contain (per Johner Institut best practice):

### Required Fields

| Field | ISO 14971 Reference | Description |
|-------|-------------------|-------------|
| **FMEA-ID** | — | Unique identifier: `FMEA-NNN` |
| **REQ-ID** | Traceability | Linked requirement(s) |
| **Function** | §5.2 | System function being analyzed |
| **Hazard (HAZ-ID)** | §5.3 | Potential source of harm (link to hazard catalog) |
| **Sequence of Events** | Annex C | How hazard leads to hazardous situation |
| **Hazardous Situation** | §3.5 | Circumstances of exposure |
| **Harm** | §3.2 | Specific injury or damage |
| **Severity (S)** | §5.4 | 1-10 per acceptance matrix |
| **P1** (prob. of hazardous situation) | Annex C | Likelihood of situation occurring |
| **P2** (prob. of harm from situation) | Annex C | Likelihood of harm given situation |
| **Probability (P = P1 × P2)** | Annex C | Combined probability category (1-5) |
| **Initial Risk Level** | §5.4 | From acceptance matrix (Accept/ALARP/Unaccept) |
| **Risk Control Measure** | §6 | Concrete measure to reduce risk |
| **Control Hierarchy Level** | §6.2 | Inherent Design / Protective / Information |
| **Residual Severity** | §7.1 | Severity after control |
| **Residual P1 / P2** | §7.1 | Probability after control |
| **Residual Risk Level** | §7.1 | After control (Accept/ALARP/Unaccept) |
| **Verification Method** | §6.3 | How to verify control works |
| **Verification REQ-ID** | Traceability | New safety requirement (if control creates one) |
| **Verification Test-ID** | Traceability | Test case that verifies the control |
| **Benefit-Risk Analysis** | §7.2 | For ALARP residual risks: why benefit outweighs |
| **Status** | — | Open / Controlled / Accepted / Verified |

### FMEA Quality Rules (Johner Institut)

- ❌ **DO NOT** use RPN as primary decision criterion — RPN is not ISO 14971 conformant
- ✅ Use the 5×10 acceptance matrix with Accept / ALARP / Unaccept regions
- ✅ ALARP decisions must be justified in the Benefit-Risk Analysis field
- ✅ Every FMEA entry must have a linked REQ-ID
- ✅ Every risk control must have a linked verification Test-ID
- ✅ Risk controls must follow the hierarchy: Design → Protection → Information
- ✅ Information as sole control is NOT acceptable for Severity ≥ 8

## 4. Hazard Identification List Structure

Generated by RE agent from requirements, refined by Risk Manager:

```markdown
# Hazard Identification List

## HAZ-001: [Hazard Name]
- **Category**: Patient Safety / Operator Safety / Data / Software / Use Error / Environmental
- **Source Requirement(s)**: REQ-XXX
- **Hazard Description**: [What is the potential source of harm?]
- **Hazardous Situation(s)**: [Under what circumstances?]
- **Potential Harm(s)**: [What injury/damage?]
- **Initial Severity Assessment**: X/10
- **Linked FMEA**: FMEA-NNN
- **Linked Risk Controls**: [Control measures from FMEA]
```

## 5. Risk Management File — Required Contents (ISO 14971 §5)

The Risk Management File is the **audit-ready collection of ALL risk evidence**:

- [ ] Risk Management Plan
- [ ] Hazard Identification List
- [ ] System FMEA
- [ ] Software FMEA
- [ ] Process FMEA (manufacturing/service processes)
- [ ] Risk Analysis Report (summary of all analyses)
- [ ] Risk Control Verification Records (test results demonstrating control effectiveness)
- [ ] Overall Residual Risk Evaluation
- [ ] Risk Management Report (final sign-off)
- [ ] Post-Market Surveillance Plan (risk aspects)
- [ ] Production and Post-Production Information Review Records

## 6. Risk Acceptance Matrix (Standard)

This is the **default** acceptance matrix. The project may customize it in the RMP.

```
              PROBABILITY (P = P1 × P2) →
SEVERITY      1(Improb)   2(Remote)   3(Occas)    4(Prob)    5(Freq)
↓
10 Fatal       ALARP       UNACCEPT    UNACCEPT    UNACCEPT   UNACCEPT
 9 Catastr     ACCEPT      ALARP       UNACCEPT    UNACCEPT   UNACCEPT
 8 Critical    ACCEPT      ALARP       ALARP       UNACCEPT   UNACCEPT
 7 Major       ACCEPT      ACCEPT      ALARP       ALARP      UNACCEPT
 6 Significant ACCEPT      ACCEPT      ACCEPT      ALARP      ALARP
 5 Moderate    ACCEPT      ACCEPT      ACCEPT      ACCEPT     ALARP
 4 Low         ACCEPT      ACCEPT      ACCEPT      ACCEPT     ACCEPT
 3 Slight      ACCEPT      ACCEPT      ACCEPT      ACCEPT     ACCEPT
 2 Minor       ACCEPT      ACCEPT      ACCEPT      ACCEPT     ACCEPT
 1 Negligible  ACCEPT      ACCEPT      ACCEPT      ACCEPT     ACCEPT
```

### Definition of Regions
- **ACCEPT**: Risk acceptable. Document rationale. Monitor post-market.
- **ALARP**: As Low As Reasonably Practicable. Risk must be reduced further unless benefit-risk analysis justifies. Additional controls only if benefit outweighs cost/feasibility of control.
- **UNACCEPT**: Risk unacceptable. MUST be reduced. Cannot release product with unacceptable risks.

---

## 7. Overall Residual Risk Evaluation (ISO 14971 §8)

Before product release, the Risk Manager must evaluate:
- [ ] All individual residual risks are acceptable or ALARP-justified
- [ ] No unacceptable risks remain
- [ ] The overall residual risk (combination of all individual residual risks) is acceptable
- [ ] Benefits of the device outweigh the overall residual risk
- [ ] Risk management report is signed by authorized person

---

## 8. Post-Market Risk Management (ISO 14971 §9, §10)

- [ ] Post-Market Surveillance Plan includes risk monitoring
- [ ] Process for collecting production and post-production information
- [ ] Process for reviewing information for relevance to safety
- [ ] Process for updating risk management file when new hazards are identified
- [ ] Vigilance reporting process (serious incidents → authorities)
- [ ] Field Safety Corrective Action (FSCA) process

---

## 9. Traceability Requirements

Every risk management element must trace:

```
REQ-ID ←→ FMEA-ID ←→ HAZ-ID ←→ Risk Control ←→ Safety REQ-ID ←→ Test-ID
```

- **REQ → FMEA**: Every requirement has hazard analysis
- **FMEA → HAZ**: Every FMEA entry links to a hazard
- **Risk Control → Safety REQ**: Controls that become requirements
- **Safety REQ → Test**: Every safety requirement has a verification test
- **Test → Pass/Fail**: Every verification test has a result

The traceability matrix at `project/10_Documentation/TraceabilityMatrix.md` must include all these links.
