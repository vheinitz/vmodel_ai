# ADR-003: Vortex Mixing Principle

## Decision
The mixing mechanism shall use the vortex principle: an eccentrically mounted cup driven by a DC motor, mixing one tube at a time via contact pressure.

## Context
The stakeholder needs describe a "small, simple" mixer for "standard lab tubes or small containers." Key requirements:
- REQ-SYS-010: Uniform mixing (CV ≤ 5%)
- REQ-SYS-020: Accept standard tubes (Ø 13–30 mm)
- REQ-SYS-060: Cleanable in ≤ 2 minutes

## Alternatives Considered

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| **Vortex** (chosen) | Simple mechanics, easy to clean (single cup), small footprint, proven in labs worldwide | Single tube at a time, operator must press tube into cup (or use holder) |
| Orbital Shaker | Multiple tubes simultaneously, hands-free operation | Larger, more complex mechanics, harder to clean (platform with tube holes), higher cost |
| Rocking Platform | Gentle mixing for sensitive samples | Slow, not suitable for vortex-level mixing, larger footprint |

## Consequences
- **Positive**: Minimal mechanical parts (motor + eccentric cup + spring holder), easy to decontaminate (single smooth cup surface), small device footprint
- **Positive**: Vortex mixing is a well-characterized process — mixing quality vs. speed/time is predictable
- **Negative**: Single-tube throughput — if lab needs to mix many tubes quickly, operator must process sequentially (~30 seconds per tube including loading)
- **Accepted**: Single-tube operation aligns with stakeholder need for "simple" and "small"
