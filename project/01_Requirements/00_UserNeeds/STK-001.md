# Uniform and Consistent Liquid Mixing

## Need

The device shall mix patient samples, reagents, and other medical solutions evenly and consistently every time it is operated. The mixing result must be reproducible across multiple runs so that laboratory staff can trust the diagnostic outcomes derived from the mixed liquids.

## Context

In a medical laboratory, liquid mixing is a routine preparatory step before analysis. Uneven mixing can cause concentration gradients in the sample, leading to measurement errors in downstream diagnostic instruments. This need applies to daily lab workflows where multiple samples are processed sequentially.

## Acceptance

Laboratory staff will consider this need met when:
- Visual inspection shows homogeneous mixing with no visible layering or sedimentation
- Repeated mixing of identical samples produces consistent diagnostic test results within expected tolerance
